Travel Essentials

Important information to prepare for your trip

Visa Requirements

Most visitors to Sri Lanka need a visa. You can apply for an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) online before your trip. The process is simple and usually takes 24-48 hours.

Apply for ETA

Currency

The currency of Sri Lanka is the Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). ATMs are widely available in cities and tourist areas. Credit cards are accepted at most hotels and larger restaurants, but it's good to carry cash for smaller establishments and rural areas.

Electricity

Sri Lanka uses 230V, 50Hz electricity. Power sockets are type D, M, and G. It's recommended to bring a universal adapter for your electronic devices.

Language

Sinhala and Tamil are the official languages of Sri Lanka. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and by many locals, especially in urban areas.

Useful Phrases:

  • Hello: Ayubowan (Sinhala)
  • Thank you: Istuti (Sinhala)
  • Yes/No: Ow/Nae (Sinhala)
  • How much?: Keeyada? (Sinhala)

Transportation in Sri Lanka

How to get around the island

Trains

Train travel in Sri Lanka is an experience in itself, especially the scenic routes through the hill country. The Kandy to Ella train journey is considered one of the most beautiful train rides in the world.

Train Schedules & Booking

Buses

Local buses are the cheapest way to travel around Sri Lanka. They cover almost every corner of the island but can be crowded and don't always follow strict schedules.

Private Car & Driver

Hiring a private car with a driver is a popular and convenient option for tourists. It gives you flexibility and comfort, especially when traveling with family or a group.
